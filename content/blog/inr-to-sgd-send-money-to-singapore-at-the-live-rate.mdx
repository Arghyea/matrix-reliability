## Introduction

Singapore is one of the most concentrated forex destinations from India.

A small geographical area, a high-trust banking system, two world-ranked universities, a business hub for half of Asia, and a popular short-trip destination. An Indian wiring SGD might be paying NUS or NTU tuition, supporting a sibling working at a Singapore tech firm, paying for a daughter's wedding photography in Sentosa, or simply loading a forex card for a long weekend at Changi.

Behind all of these transactions is the same question.

What should the SGD actually cost in rupees, and how much of what the bank quotes is the real rate versus the markup? This guide answers that, with a focus on the specific quirks that make SGD different from USD or GBP.

## How the Singapore Dollar is managed (and why it matters for your rate)

The SGD is not freely floating like the GBP, not pegged like the AED, but managed inside an undisclosed band against a basket of currencies of Singapore's major trading partners.

The Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) is the central bank, and it adjusts the band's slope and width in periodic monetary policy reviews, typically twice a year in April and October.

The practical effect: the SGD is generally more stable than freely-floating currencies of similar-sized economies. It moves, but it moves less, and it moves in directions broadly aligned with MAS's policy stance. For a remitter, this means SGD-INR has lower week-to-week volatility than EUR-INR or GBP-INR.

It does not mean the rate is fixed.

SGD-USD moves daily on global flows, and USD-INR moves on Indian factors. The cross, SGD-INR, moves with both. But the volatility is dampened.

## How the INR to SGD cross-rate is built

Same structure as most non-USD currencies for an Indian remitter.

The INR-SGD rate is built as a cross. You take SGD-USD and combine it with USD-INR. The Singapore dollar's price in US dollars is set by the global market, with MAS managing the SGD inside its band. The dollar's price in rupees is set by the interbank market in India. Multiply the two and you get the live INR per Singapore dollar figure that Google publishes.

When MAS adjusts policy or signals a stance change, SGD-USD moves, and the cross moves with it. When the Indian rupee weakens, SGD costs more rupees even if SGD-USD is unchanged.

## The markup on SGD wires: slightly less than GBP, slightly more than USD

Retail bank markup on SGD wires from India sits in between.

Slightly less than GBP or EUR, because SGD is more stable, so the bank takes less compensation for volatility risk. Slightly more than USD, because SGD volume is lower than USD volume on most Indian corridors.

On an SGD 30,000 tuition wire, which is roughly 19 lakh rupees, the markup difference between a retail bank and an authorised dealer adds up to a sum the family genuinely notices.

The way to test a quote is the same as for any currency. Google 1 SGD to INR for the live mid-market, multiply by your SGD amount, compare to the quote. The gap is the cost.

## The four ways an Indian remits SGD

Each has its own mechanics and timing.

## Tuition wire to NUS, NTU, SMU, or other Singapore universities

NUS and NTU are world-ranked and increasingly attractive to Indian students.

They accept tuition by SWIFT wire to the university's treasury. Wire instructions appear in the student's portal. Beneficiary, SWIFT code, account number, reference (the student matriculation ID).

Singapore tuition for international students is meaningfully higher than for Singaporean citizens but lower than US private universities. The wire size sits in a sweet spot where the markup difference is large enough to matter, but small enough that retail remitters often do not optimise.

## Family maintenance to a relative in Singapore

A parent supporting a child working in Singapore, often in tech, finance, or shipping.

Family supporting an elderly parent who has moved to be with adult children.

These are LRS-permitted maintenance remittances. Wire to the relative's Singapore bank account using the local clearing details and SWIFT routing. Recipient typically receives the SGD within one to two working days.

## Property purchase or investment in Singapore

Singapore property is a popular investment for affluent Indians.

With one important caveat: high stamp duties for foreign buyers. The Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty for foreign nationals can be substantial, so verify the current rate with a Singapore-side agent before committing.

The wire mechanics are the same as any LRS investment remittance. A2 form, sale agreement, lawyer letter, and the wire to the seller's solicitor escrow account.

## Travel forex: SGD cash and forex card for a Singapore trip

For a Singapore trip, load SGD on a multi-currency forex card and carry a small amount of cash.

Singapore is heavily card-based. Every hawker stall, every MRT station, every taxi accepts contactless or PayNow QR. The cash is for the rare exception and for tipping, which is not customary in Singapore but exists at restaurants.

A typical short Singapore trip on a forex card costs a few hundred to a couple of thousand Singapore dollars depending on accommodation choice. Established RBI Category-II authorised dealers like Matrix Forex offer multi-currency cards with SGD as a standard wallet, loaded close to the live mid-market rate.

## SGD-specific things worth knowing

Singapore is largely cashless. Among Asian travel destinations, Singapore is one of the most cashless. PayNow QR codes, contactless cards, and mobile wallets dominate. A forex card with NFC works at almost every terminal. Heavy cash carrying is unnecessary.

Singapore IRAS and tax residency. For Indians moving to Singapore for work, tax residency in Singapore typically begins after 183 days of physical presence in a tax year. For pure family-maintenance wires from India, the Singapore tax position of the recipient does not change the Indian compliance. The A2 form and the LRS purpose are the only required pieces.

TCS on SGD remittance. The rules apply to all LRS remittances regardless of currency. Education through a recognised loan is zero. Self-funded education above 10 lakh attracts 2 per cent on the part above. Other LRS purposes above 10 lakh attract 20 per cent on the part above. Confirm the prevailing rate at the time of remittance, since these have moved.

SGD cash carry. RBI permits up to USD 2,000 equivalent in cash per trip, roughly 2,700 Singapore dollars. Most travellers carry a couple of hundred dollars in cash and the rest on a forex card.

Reload during travel. A multi-currency forex card with an SGD wallet can be topped up from India during the trip. A family member in India can arrange the top-up through the branch. Useful for unplanned spending or extended stays.

## Putting It All Together

The Singapore Dollar is more stable than most freely-floating currencies, because MAS manages it inside a band rather than letting it float freely.

But the retail bank quote on an SGD wire still includes a markup that you do not see as a line item. Compare against Google's live cross-rate before any wire.

Use an RBI-authorised dealer for the narrow spread on the underlying mid-market. Keep the A2 form, admission letter or relationship proof, beneficiary details, and PAN ready.

The savings on a single NUS or NTU tuition wire are enough to pay for a Changi-bound flight. The savings across a four-year programme are enough to pay for several.

## Frequently asked questions about INR to SGD

**What is the live INR to SGD exchange rate today?**

The rate moves continuously, though typically with lower volatility than INR to GBP or EUR because MAS manages SGD inside an undisclosed band. Search 1 SGD to INR on Google Finance or for the live mid-market rate. Any retail quote adds a markup. An authorised dealer typically quotes closer to the live rate than a retail bank.

**How is the Singapore Dollar managed and why does it matter for my rate?**

The SGD is managed by the Monetary Authority of Singapore inside an undisclosed band against a basket of trading-partner currencies. MAS adjusts the band's slope and width twice a year. This makes SGD generally more stable than freely-floating currencies, so SGD-INR has lower week-to-week volatility than GBP-INR or EUR-INR. It does not make the rate fixed. It just dampens the volatility.

**How do I send SGD to NUS or NTU for tuition?**

Get the wire instructions from the student's admission portal. Beneficiary name, SWIFT code, account number, and student-ID reference. Take these to an authorised dealer with the A2 form, admission letter, and PAN. The wire goes via SWIFT and reaches the university in one to two working days.

**Can I open a Singapore bank account from India before I move?**

Most Singapore banks (DBS, OCBC, UOB, Standard Chartered) require physical presence to open an account. A handful of banks allow remote opening for incoming students or work-permit holders against documentation, but it is the exception rather than the rule. Plan to open the local account within the first two weeks of arrival.

**Is TCS applicable on SGD remittance for Singapore tuition?**

Yes, TCS applies to all LRS remittances regardless of currency. Education via a recognised education loan is zero. Self-funded education is zero up to 10 lakh in a financial year and 2 per cent on the amount above. Confirm the current rate with the authorised dealer before initiating the wire.

**How long does an INR to SGD wire take?**

A correctly documented SWIFT wire from an Indian authorised dealer typically reaches a Singapore beneficiary in one to two working days. Singapore's banking infrastructure is among the fastest in Asia. Wires sent on Friday after cutoff typically settle on Monday or Tuesday.

**Should I load SGD on a forex card or carry cash for a Singapore trip?**

Singapore is heavily cashless. Load SGD on a multi-currency forex card with NFC and use it for almost everything. Carry a couple of hundred Singapore dollars in cash for incidentals and the rare cash-only vendor. The forex card rate, set at the time of loading, is locked in for the duration of the card's use.

**Can I send SGD for Singapore property purchase under LRS?**

Yes, property investment is a permitted LRS purpose, subject to the USD 250,000 annual limit per individual and Singapore's own foreign-buyer rules, including Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty, which can be significant for foreign nationals. The wire mechanics are standard. A2 form, sale agreement, solicitor instructions, wire to the escrow account. Verify the Singapore-side stamp duty position with a local agent before committing.
