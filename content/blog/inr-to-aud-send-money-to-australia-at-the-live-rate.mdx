## Introduction

Australia sits in the top three study-abroad destinations for Indian students, alongside the US and Canada.

The 500 student visa stream is straightforward but cash-intensive. The applicant has to demonstrate funds for tuition, twelve months of living expenses, and Overseas Student Health Cover. The Genuine Student criterion has replaced the older Genuine Temporary Entrant framework as the assessment lens, but the financial demonstration remains.

For a family budgeting an Australian degree, the AUD figures feel manageable on paper.

The forex on the way to Australia is where the math quietly goes wrong, because the same family that obsesses over fees and tuition often pays the retail bank markup on the wires themselves without seeing it. This guide walks through the four AUD wires a typical Indian family makes, the AUD-specific quirks worth knowing, and the small set of moves that consistently get a better rate.

## How the INR to AUD rate is built

The Australian Dollar is freely floating, and its drivers are unusual.

Iron ore prices. Coal prices. China's growth trajectory. The Reserve Bank of Australia's interest rate decisions. These together make the AUD one of the more volatile major currencies for Indian remitters.

The INR to AUD rate is built as a cross. You take the AUD-USD rate and combine it with USD-INR. The Australian dollar's price in US dollars moves with iron ore, coal, China growth signals, and RBA policy. The dollar's price in rupees is set by the interbank market in India. Multiply the two and you get the live INR per Australian dollar figure that Google publishes.

When iron ore moves sharply, AUD-USD moves with it, and the cross moves with both legs.

Movement of two or three per cent per month in AUD-USD is normal. This translates directly to AUD-INR, which is why a family budgeting in January for a March tuition wire can find the rupee cost has shifted noticeably by the time the wire actually goes out.

## The four AUD wires an Indian family typically makes

These come in sequence over the planning year and the first year of the programme.

## Tuition wire to an Australian university

Australian universities like the Group of Eight (Sydney, Melbourne, ANU, UNSW, UQ, UWA, Monash, Adelaide) and the broader system accept tuition by SWIFT wire.

The wire goes either to the university's treasury account directly, or via a payment partner like PayMyTuition, Western Union Convera, or Flywire.

Wire instructions appear in the student's admission portal. Beneficiary name, BSB number which is the Australian bank routing identifier, account number, SWIFT or BIC code, and the reference number, which is usually the student ID.

International undergraduate tuition runs to several tens of thousands of Australian dollars per year. A three-year degree builds to a substantial cumulative figure. The markup difference per wire, repeated across the journey, adds up to a meaningful share of one term's tuition by graduation.

## OSHC payment for the 500 visa

Overseas Student Health Cover is mandatory for the duration of the 500 visa.

Major OSHC providers are Allianz, BUPA, Medibank, and NIB. They quote in Australian dollars and accept payment by SWIFT wire or international credit card.

A 12-month OSHC for a single student runs in the low hundreds of dollars. For a couple or family, the figure is several times higher depending on coverage.

For smaller amounts, paying with an Indian credit card is often cleaner than a SWIFT wire, since the markup difference is smaller and there is no SWIFT fee. For larger family OSHC packages, a SWIFT wire through an authorised dealer is more economical.

## Proof of living-expense funds or pre-arrival deposit

The 500 visa requires demonstrated living-expense funds for twelve months.

The Department of Home Affairs publishes this amount and adjusts it periodically. Verify the current figure on their website before applying.

For most applicants, the funds are demonstrated through bank statements showing the equivalent rupee balance. Some students choose to wire a portion of the living-expense fund to an Australian bank account opened pre-arrival, particularly with banks like Commonwealth Bank or NAB that offer remote opening for incoming international students.

This is not strictly required, but it eases the early days post-arrival.

## Living-expense top-ups during the programme

Once the student is in Australia and has a local bank account and (typically) part-time work, the family's ongoing remittances become smaller and more frequent.

These are family-maintenance wires under LRS to the student's Australian account, or alternatively the family loads the student's forex card from India.

Both approaches are valid. The forex card load avoids the SWIFT fee on each small remittance, but the wire to the Australian account integrates more cleanly into the student's day-to-day banking.

## Family maintenance and travel forex to Australia

Beyond students, AUD remittances cover family support to relatives working in Australia (Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane all have substantial Indian communities), family on PR or work visas, and travel forex for tourists.

For travel, an AUD forex card with a cash supplement of a couple of hundred dollars covers most needs. Australia is largely card-friendly, especially in cities. Rural areas and small towns sometimes prefer cash.

Tipping is uncommon. The bill is the bill.

## AUD-specific things worth knowing

BSB number is the Australian routing identifier. Australian bank accounts are identified by a BSB (Bank-State-Branch) number of six digits, plus the account number. For SWIFT wires from India, the SWIFT or BIC code plus the account number is what you need. The BSB is for domestic Australian transfers.

Australian banking is accessible from India. Major Australian banks like Commonwealth Bank, NAB, Westpac, and ANZ allow remote opening for incoming international students against the visa grant or admission letter. The student can have an Australian account ready before they fly, which eases arrival logistics.

TCS on AUD remittance. The rules apply uniformly across LRS purposes regardless of currency. Education through a recognised education loan is zero. Self-funded education above 10 lakh attracts 2 per cent on the part above. Other LRS purposes above 10 lakh attract 20 per cent on the part above. A year of tuition in AUD usually crosses 10 lakh in rupees, so for self-funded families some TCS applies on the part above the threshold.

AUD cash carry. RBI permits up to USD 2,000 equivalent in cash per trip, which is roughly 3,000 Australian dollars. Most travellers carry a couple of hundred dollars in cash and load the rest on a forex card.

Genuine Student criteria and financial documentation. The 500 visa now uses Genuine Student assessment, replacing the earlier Genuine Temporary Entrant framework. Financial demonstration remains a core component. Bank statements, education-loan sanction letters, and CA-certified income statements are typical evidence.

For wires through an RBI Category-II authorised dealer like Matrix Forex, the rate sits closer to the live cross-rate than retail bank quotes, and the dealer's familiarity with Australian university wires means the documentation set is handled as routine.

## Putting It All Together

AUD is more volatile than most major currencies because it sits at the intersection of commodity prices and China growth.

For a three-year Australian degree with multiple tuition wires, OSHC payments, and ongoing family top-ups, the cumulative markup difference between a retail bank and an authorised dealer adds up to a real share of the family's budget, often enough to cover a return flight home.

Watch the live cross-rate. Use an authorised dealer for the spread. Open the Australian bank account remotely before flying. Keep the A2 form, admission letter, and PAN ready as the documentation backbone.

The forex side of an Australian degree should be the cheapest part of it, not the part that quietly costs the most.

## Frequently asked questions about INR to AUD

**What is the live INR to AUD exchange rate today?**

The live rate moves continuously and tracks iron ore prices, China growth signals, and RBA policy. Search 1 AUD to INR on Google Finance for the live mid-market rate. Any retail quote adds a markup. An authorised dealer typically quotes much closer to the live rate than a retail bank.

**How much money do I need to show for an Australia 500 student visa?**

Tuition for the first year, twelve months of living expenses at the figure published by the Department of Home Affairs, OSHC for the visa duration, and return airfare. Verify the current living-expense amount on the Department of Home Affairs website before applying, since these are adjusted upward over time.

**How do I send AUD to an Australian university for tuition?**

Get the wire instructions from the student's admission portal. Beneficiary name, BSB number, account number, SWIFT or BIC code, and the student-ID reference. Take these to an authorised dealer with the A2 form, admission letter, and PAN. The wire goes via SWIFT and reaches the university in one to three working days.

**Can I open an Australian bank account from India before I arrive?**

Yes. Major Australian banks like Commonwealth Bank, NAB, Westpac, and ANZ allow remote account opening for incoming international students against the visa grant or admission letter. The account is opened online. The debit card is collected after arrival. This makes the early days post-arrival significantly easier.

**What is OSHC and how do I pay for it from India?**

OSHC, or Overseas Student Health Cover, is mandatory health insurance for 500 visa holders. Major providers are Allianz, BUPA, Medibank, and NIB. Payment can be made via SWIFT wire or international credit card. For amounts in the low hundreds of dollars, an Indian credit card payment is usually simplest. For larger family OSHC packages, a SWIFT wire through an authorised dealer is more economical.

**Is TCS applicable on AUD remittance for Australian tuition?**

Yes, TCS rules apply to all LRS remittances regardless of currency. Education via a recognised education loan is zero. Self-funded education is zero up to 10 lakh in a financial year and 2 per cent on the amount above. Confirm the current rate before initiating the wire.

**How long does an INR to AUD wire take?**

A correctly documented SWIFT wire from an Indian authorised dealer typically reaches an Australian beneficiary in one to three working days. Wires sent on Friday after Indian cutoff usually settle on Tuesday or Wednesday Australian time, accounting for the time difference.

**Why does the AUD rate fluctuate so much compared to the USD?** AUD is a commodity currency. Its rate is heavily influenced by iron ore and coal prices and by China's growth. A sharp move in iron ore prices over a month can shift AUD-USD noticeably, which directly moves AUD-INR. USD-INR moves on Indian factors. The cross-rate AUD-INR therefore inherits both volatilities.
