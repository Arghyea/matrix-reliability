If you're paying overseas tuition, supporting family abroad, or funding travel, you'll likely use the **Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)**. Here's what to know before you send.

## What LRS allows

Under LRS, a resident individual can remit up to a set annual limit for permitted purposes — including education, maintenance of relatives, medical treatment, and travel. The exact ceiling and tax treatment are set by the RBI and can change, so always confirm the current figure.

## Documents you'll typically need

- PAN card
- Purpose declaration (Form A2)
- Supporting documents for the purpose (e.g. university invoice for tuition)

## Tax collected at source (TCS)

Remittances above certain thresholds may attract **TCS**, which you can usually adjust against your income tax. Education-linked remittances funded by a loan are often treated more favourably. Check the latest rules or ask us.
