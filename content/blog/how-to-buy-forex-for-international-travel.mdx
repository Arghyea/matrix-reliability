Buying foreign currency should be simple, but the way most counters quote rates makes it confusing on purpose. Here's how to get it right.

## Understand the rate you're actually paying

Every forex provider starts from the **interbank rate** (the "real" mid-market rate) and adds a margin. The headline number isn't what matters — the **all-in rate after markup** is. Always ask for the final per-unit rate, including any service charge.

> Tip: If a quote looks too good, check whether GST and handling fees are bundled in or added later.

## Cash, card, or both?

- **Cash** is essential for tips, taxis, and small vendors.
- **A forex card** locks your rate the day you load it and is safer to carry.
- Most travellers do best with a **split** — a little cash, the rest on card.

## Avoid the airport

Airport counters carry the widest margins, full stop. Order ahead online and either collect or get it delivered. You'll almost always do better.

## Time it sensibly

You can't predict currency markets, and trying to is a losing game for a holiday budget. If the rate is reasonable and your trip is close, lock it in rather than gambling on a better number next week.
